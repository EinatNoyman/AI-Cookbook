## Executive Summary

A centralized **Angular Developer Hub** will serve as the engineering team's single source of truth: a clear, intuitive home for links, guidelines, and workflows, plus a **living AI Cookbook** of curated code snippets and prompts for Cursor, Bolt, and Claude Code. It addresses the cognitive load and duplication caused by scattered Confluence content and the absence of structured AI standards—enabling developers to adopt AI-assisted workflows instead of remaining stuck in manual "black work" and leaving the organization behind technologically.

---

## Core Vision

### Problem Statement

Today, knowledge and standards are scattered. Confluence is messy and repetitive because developers don't know what others have already written; finding the right information is difficult. There is no single place that defines structured workflows or standards for using AI platforms. As a result, many developers underuse or avoid AI tools, the organization falls behind technically, and people still do a lot of manual, repetitive work—which is costly and slows delivery.

### Problem Impact

- **Cognitive load:** Time and mental energy wasted searching and re-reading overlapping content.
- **Reinventing the wheel:** Duplicate or conflicting guidance when people don't know what already exists.
- **Low AI adoption:** Without clear, centralized standards and examples, developers don't adopt AI-assisted workflows.
- **Technical debt and cost:** The org lags; "black work" remains high and delivery velocity stays lower than it could be.

### Why Existing Solutions Fall Short

Confluence and similar wikis are generic and document-centric. They weren't designed as a developer-first, AI-first hub. Content becomes messy and repetitive, discovery is poor, and there is no dedicated space for AI workflows, prompts, and standards—so they don't effectively pull developers toward modern AI tools or make usage simple and consistent.

### Proposed Solution

An **Angular-based Developer Hub** that is:

- **Centralized:** One place for links, guidelines, workflows, and standards.
- **Structured and clear:** Simple, intuitive, and organized so information is easy to find and reuse.
- **A living AI Cookbook:** Curated, high-quality code snippets and optimized prompts for Cursor, Bolt, Claude Code, and similar platforms, with best practices and standards for AI-assisted development.
- **Engaging:** Interactive and feature-rich enough to attract developers and encourage them to explore and use AI tools, moving the whole team toward a high-velocity, AI-augmented workflow.

### Key Differentiators

- **Developer- and AI-first:** Built for how developers work and for AI-assisted workflows, not as a generic wiki.
- **One source of truth:** Reduces duplication and "lost" links; everyone can build on the same standards and examples.
- **Designed for adoption:** Clear structure plus interactive, "cool" features to draw developers in and make AI usage feel natural and supported.
- **Tailored to the team:** Curated by and for your engineering org, so it reflects your stack, your patterns, and your AI tooling (Cursor, Bolt, Claude Code, Qodo, Copilot).

---

## Target Users

### Primary Users

**All developers** — One primary audience with one shared usage pattern. The hub is for every engineer on the team: same entry point, same navigation, same core actions (find links, read guidelines, use the AI Cookbook). No separate flows for different roles. Design principle: **super intuitive and simple, but genuinely useful** — minimal cognitive load, clear structure, and immediate value so anyone can use it without training.

### Secondary Users

Not defined for this product. The focus is a single, unified experience for all developers.

### User Journey

- **Discovery:** Developers learn about the hub as the team's default place for links, guidelines, and AI prompts (onboarding, word of mouth, or team communication).
- **Onboarding:** First use is straightforward: open the app, see a clear layout (e.g. links, guidelines, AI Cookbook), no complex setup.
- **Core usage:** Daily use is simple: find a link, check a standard, or copy/use a prompt or snippet from the AI Cookbook. Same flow for everyone.
- **Success moment:** "I found what I needed quickly" and "I actually used an AI prompt from the hub" — low friction, high usefulness.
- **Long-term:** The hub becomes the default place to look before Confluence or ad-hoc search; AI Cookbook usage becomes part of normal workflow.

---

## Success Metrics

**User success:** Developers find what they need quickly; they use the AI Cookbook in real work; they say "this was worth it." Behaviors that indicate value: weekly (or regular) visits, copying or using prompts/snippets, and using the hub during onboarding.

**Business / team success:** Less time lost searching Confluence; higher adoption of AI-assisted workflows; fewer duplicate or conflicting guidelines; the hub becomes the default "first place to look."

### Business Objectives

- **Short term (e.g. 3 months):** Hub is the go-to place for links and guidelines; AI Cookbook has meaningful content and is discoverable; developers use it without extra training.
- **Medium term (e.g. 12 months):** Measurable reduction in "where's that link?" and duplicate docs; increased and sustained use of AI tools (Cursor, Bolt, Claude Code, etc.) supported by hub standards and prompts.

### Key Performance Indicators

- **Engagement:** e.g. X% of developers use the hub at least weekly; Y visits or actions per active user per month.
- **AI Cookbook usage:** e.g. Z cookbook prompts or snippets used/copied per month (or per release).
- **Adoption / behavior:** e.g. new joiners use the hub in first week; reduction in Confluence searches for developer-facing links/guidelines (if measurable).
- **Outcome:** Qualitative signal that "black work" is decreasing and AI-augmented workflow is becoming normal (e.g. team surveys, anecdotal feedback).
